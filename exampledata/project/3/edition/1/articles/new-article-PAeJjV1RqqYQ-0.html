Introduction

In the latter half of the 17th century, the Diaconie Weeshuis, an orphanage managed by a protestant church, proudly overlooked the Amstel river. It was located on former ‘Vlooienburg’, an island constructed in the Amstel for urban expansion around 1600. The orphanage was constructed in 1656, and stood for 230 years before it got replaced by a new building in Neogothic style in 1888. In the 1980s the entire neighbourhood was demolished to make space for the new Amsterdam city hall and opera (nick named ‘stopera’).
