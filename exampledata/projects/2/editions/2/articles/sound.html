How Does It Make a Sound?

Handle, which is rarely found in other instruments, is one of the unique point to this instrument. When the player turns the handle, the rosined wheel spins inside the instrument, rubbing against the strings and making sound. By minute interruptions of the wheelâ€™s rotation a clearly articulated rhythm can be produced without disturbing the melody[Baines2001]. This technic is called "Trompette".  The rotation of the handle is merely for sound; it also creates rhythm.
